Our Logotype

The various forms of our logo, and when to use each one.

The YSH Logo features a versatile, modern and clean icon coupled with a fun and curvy yet professional font. It creates a look that doesn't scream "too fun" or "too professional."


1 - The logotype: Use this whenever possible.

2 - The Glyph: Use this in tight spaces, or when a full logo type is not necessary.

3 - Stripped Lockup: Without subset text, use when subset becomes a
readability issue.

Typography

The fonts of YSH 2016.

The YSH typographic scheme features only one font. The power of typographic contrast in terms of size and scale creates easy readability and smooth textual flow.

Contrasting fonts is done by decreasing the size based on the nature of the given text (heading, sub-heading, sub-titles, etc) and, most importantly, by decreasing weights accordingly for heading-related texts as displayed below.

FONT: GT WALSHEIM:

PROFILE: GT Walsheim is smooth, curvy and fun font. It resembles more serious fonts such as Gotham yet features several quirky curves giving it a less serious, more fun appearance.

Spacing

Important spacing factors to take into account.

When using the logo in designs with other elements, it is crucial that there is proper spacing and white space surrounding the logo. It is suggested to keep a minimum of one glyph size around the logo and other elements.


When using only the glyph, keep a reasonable amount of space (~35%) between it and other elements. Your approximation is totally fine.
